# Highline Ridge — Storyboard Planning

> Local planning document. Gritty branch ideas built from current rooms, conversations, stats, and tone.
> **Canon so far:** amnesiac arrival, lavish cabin, Highline ridge settlement, Graysmill valley below, saloon loop, lucidity collapse, wallet with $20 and an unread slip of paper.

---

## Core Mystery (Working Theory)

You are not merely lost. You are **between versions of yourself** — a man who keeps returning to the cabin floor with ash on his tongue and bourbon in his blood, asking the same questions in the same order while the mountain pretends it has never met you before.

The game is not about *where* you are. It is about **what you did on the last pass** and whether this pass will be the one where you finally read the slip of paper instead of folding it back into the seam.

---

## Path A — *The Loop That Learns*

**Premise:** Time on the Highline is not linear. It is **iterative**. Each lucidity collapse is not death — it is a soft reset with partial environmental persistence (fire still warm, espresso machine still hot, cowboy still tired of you).

### Beat structure

1. **Pass 1:** Player learns geography only. Cowboy, bartender, balcony women all behave as written.
2. **Pass 2:** Dialogue variants reference pass 1 without explaining why ("You asked about Graysmill already. You always do.").
3. **Pass 3+:** Small physical changes appear:
   - Persian rug path worn *deeper* in one direction.
   - Moby-Dick bookmark moved to a different madness line.
   - Saloon side passage: boot scuffs now point *out* instead of in.
4. **Disturbing turn:** On pass 5, examining the cabin after restart reveals **a second set of prints under yours** in the kitchen flour — smaller, barefoot, dragged slightly.

### End states

- **Break the loop:** Refuse the bartender's bourbon *three passes in a row*, take water, speak to burgundy woman on balcony with lucidity above 60, and examine wallet slip — reveals a name that is **yours but spelled wrong**, as if written by someone who heard it once through a door.
- **Feed the loop:** Always drink the usual. Lucidity crashes. Eventually the wake-on-floor text gains a new paragraph: you find **your own note** in your handwriting, dated tomorrow.

### Grit

The loop is not supernatural convenience. Someone **maintains** it. The espresso machine is too expensive, the rug too city-fine. The cabin is a **stage** dressed for a mind that needs comfort before it can be harvested again.

## Path B — *Graysmill Below the Clouds*

**Premise:** The valley city is not backdrop. It is **the crime scene** viewed from a courtroom built at altitude.

### Why Highline exists (historical horror)

Graysmill did not merely cut timber. It **drowned** towns when the river was redirected. It **imported** workers it later discarded. Highline's cabins were built for men who needed to dry lumber and dry their conscience — men who could watch the mill lights at night and tell themselves the smoke was weather.

### Descent routes (future rooms)

| Route | Tone | Hook |
|-------|------|------|
| Ore winch / cable tram | Industrial dread | Rusted cars still move on schedule with no operator |
| Timber slide (seasonal flume) | Body horror via logistics | Broken bones in the chute from "accidents" filed as acts of God |
| Night train (phantom schedule) | Noir | Conductor knows your face; ticket punched before you speak |

### Valley rooms to plan

- **Graysmill station:** Bulletin board of missing persons; one sketch resembles the burgundy woman without the silk.
- **Mill floor:** Ear damage as stat pressure — Tenacity checks to stay on the floor while machines scream.
- **Company boarding house:** Rows of boots; one pair still warm.
- **River bend:** Bloated timber catches on a wire — human hair snarled in the cable.

### Story question

Did you come to Highline to **investigate** Graysmill, or to **flee** what you already did there?

**Disturbing reveal option:** The wallet's $20 is not spending money. It is **hush money** from a payroll office downstairs — serial numbers match a strikebreaker fund.

## Path C — *The Narrow Path (Ridge Secrets)*

**Premise:** `narrow_path` is the honest road. The mountain road is for people who want to be seen.

### What lies up the path

1. **Charcoal burner's camp (abandoned):** Cold ring, fresh ash in the center — someone burned documents hours ago. Half a photograph: saloon interior, bartender younger, cowboy absent.
2. **Survey stake line:** Property pins for a **hotel** that was never built. Stakes carved with investor initials; one set matches the worn stamp inside your wallet.
3. **Ice house / root cellar dug into slope:** Meat hooks empty except one strip of fabric — burgundy silk, hem weighted with lead shot like the saloon curtain.
4. **The overlook behind the trees:** Not the tourist wall on High Line Road. A **private** ledge with a bench bolted to rock. Carved into the seat back: *FOR M— WAIT FOR THE FOG.*

### Horror detail

The rusted tin marker on the path, if cleaned (requires Take + Use with found lard or lamp oil), reads:

> **HANGING TREE — 1889 — 3 — COMPANY MEN — GOD MEASURES BY THE CHAIN**

The third name is scratched out so violently the tree bark beneath shows through the metal.

### Branch outcomes

- **Report to cowboy:** He finally looks at you. "You found the tree. Then you already know why I don't ride anymore."
- **Tell bartender:** He slides you bourbon without asking. Lucidity -15. "Some stories you drink to forget on purpose."
- **Follow burgundy woman after midnight (balcony speak chain):** She leads you to the path without speaking. At the tree, she says: "They hung the wrong man. He was still breathing when they cut him down. He still is."

## Path D — *Saloon Ledger*

**Premise:** The saloon is a **credit house** for sins. Everyone recognizes you because you are an account.

### NPC economy (who owns what)

| NPC | Want | Fear |
|-----|------|------|
| Bartender | Order; no police | You remembering the back room |
| Cowboy | Silence; cigarettes | The side passage hatch |
| Burgundy woman | Exit money | The company men returning |
| Emerald woman | Miner's chip stack | Being sent downstairs |
| Piano player | Finish the phrase | The song's last verse |
| Faro dealer | House edge | Player who counts cards with lucidity |

### Side passage escalation

**Examine 1:** Faro chips, pegged hatch.  
**Examine 2 (requires Tenacity):** Scratch marks on peg — someone tried from *inside*.  
**Use / Hit hatch (high Tenacity, low Health):** Splinter injury. Reveal narrow stairs down.

**Below saloon:**

- Dirt-floor room, chalk tallies on wall — names and amounts. Yours at the top, balance **paid in full** with a date two days ago (the cowboy's timeline).
- Cradle with stained blanket. Not a child's size. A **rolled rug** size.
- Locked strongbox. Key is in the wallet slip (if player ever reads it).

### Disturbing speak chain (balcony)

Burgundy → Emerald → Piano player, each unlocking one line:

1. "You paid already. Why come back?"
2. "He told us you wouldn't remember the river."
3. Piano last verse (sung under breath): *"The fog takes the face / but not the weight / Graysmill keeps the receipt."*

## Path E — *Moby-Dick as Evidence*

**Premise:** The book is not flavor. It is **annotated testimony**.

### Examine layers (multiple visits)

| Visit | Discovery |
|-------|-----------|
| 1 | Open page: wisdom/woe/madness line (current) |
| 2 | Margin notes in pencil — your handwriting, different pressure |
| 3 | Nantucket etching frame pops loose — lodge receipt for **Persian rug transport** |
| 4 | Chapter XXXIX dog-eared — Ahab's madness speech underlined; word **MONOMANIAC** circled until paper tears |
| 5 | Hollow cut behind shelf — tin of laudanum, empty except sticky ring |

### Literary-horror angle

You came to Highline quoting Melville because someone at Graysmill called you **Captain** as a joke that stuck. The whale is not a whale. It is **the mill pond** where boys were sent to clear jams.

**Use fire + book (tempting):** Burning pages restores Energy but crashes Lucidity — ash smell triggers restart with charred page fragment in inventory: one word legible, **AHAB**.

## Path F — *The Wallet Slip*

**Premise:** The slip is the game's true gate. Twenty dollars is misdirection.

### Slip text options (pick one later)

**F1 — Address only:** `GRAYSMILL — MILLHOUSE #4 — ASK FOR CORA — SAY THE CHAIN BROKE`  
**F2 — Threat:** `YOU WERE THERE WHEN THE BOILER VENTED. I KNOW THE WINDOW YOU USED.`  
**F3 — Self-address:** `If you are reading this and don't remember me, don't go back to the saloon. —M`  
**F4 — Coordinates:** Elevation + river bend notation matching mountain examine text.

### Mechanical gate

- Slip cannot be read until **Energy ≥ 40** (represents focus).
- Or requires **Espresso (kitchen Use) + Examine wallet** same pass.
- Reading slip sets Lucidity to 50 fixed — clarity hurts.

### After reading

- Bartender becomes hostile (Speak locked; Hit enabled).
- Cowboy offers cigarette — **Take** adds "Lucifer matches" item; lighting near curtain sets side passage on fire (branch).
- Inventory adds paper slip item; examining in saloon triggers scripted balcony scene.

## Path G — *Detective Exit (Procedural Noir)*

**Premise:** Player treats the game as investigation sim — examine everything, speak little, drink water, document.

### Clue board (inventory metaphor)

Collectible "notes" auto-compose when examine thresholds met:

1. Elevation + flora → place pin
2. HIGH LINE ROAD keystone date → time pin
3. Rug wear path → traffic direction
4. Saloon chip spill pattern → hurried exit from passage
5. Wallet stamp → maker in Graysmill

### Accusation scene (planned finale room)

**Graysmill courthouse** or **Highline church hall** — confront:

- **A:** Bartender ordered a killing downstairs.
- **B:** Cowboy was the killer, now paid to watch you.
- **C:** You killed someone; everyone is handler or witness.
- **D:** No single killer — the **company** vented a boiler on strikers; you survived and signed silence.

Only **D** fits all current examine facts without contradiction. Others are red herrings that still punish wrong accusation (Tenacity crash, forced restart).

## Path H — *Body Horror of Comfort*

**Premise:** Every "healing" action corrupts.

| Action | Benefit | Hidden cost |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Fire in cabin | Health +10 | Soot in lungs; later speak voice described as "rougher" |
| Espresso | Energy +30 | Heart racing; random false examine details |
| Bourbon | Tenacity +20 | Lucidity -10; collapse |
| Water | Health +5, Lucidity +10 | Bartender marks you as "difficult" — prices rise in speak trees |

### Disturbing cabin use (late game)

If player uses hearth **after** reading wallet slip, reflection in polished andirons shows **someone standing behind the chair** — description only, no NPC sprite. Next room transition: reflection gone, chair still warm.

## Path I — *Women on the Balcony (Survival Horror)*

**Premise:** They are not set dressing. They are **a warning system** for the ridge.

### Individual arcs

**Burgundy (Cora?):**  
Former mill office clerk. Knows payroll. Will trade truth for passage off the mountain. Disturbing detail: she files men's names by **teeth**, not faces — dental work from company dentist.

**Emerald:**  
Runs poker game as ledger skim. Miner's chips are loaded. If player exposes game, miner vanishes from room — examine finds only **stack of chips still warm**.

**Third woman (unnamed):**  
Speaks only when Lucidity < 25. Tells accurate future room descriptions. Player can use this to cheat navigation — at cost of permanent -5 max Health each time.

### Collective event

If player speaks on balcony three times without drinking bourbon, women synchronize — same sentence, three voices:

> "The fog is not weather. It is what the mill exhales when it sleeps."

Saloon interior ambient changes: piano phrase completes for the first time.

## Path J — *Under Construction Rooms*

**Premise:** `room_under_construction` meta — use it in-fiction.

### Narrative explanation

Some doors on the ridge open onto **rooms that are not finished for you yet** — not for the character, for **your iteration**. A handyman reality where Graysmill's future is still being nailed together.

Examine under-construction sign:

> "CLOSED FOR REPAIRS — COMPANY ORDER — DO NOT CROSS UNTIL THE WITNESS RETURNS"

Disturbing implication: **you** are the witness. The game is waiting for you to remember enough to authorize the next wing.

## Tone Guardrails

- Violence is **industrial and administrative**, not cartoon evil.
- Horror is **recognition** — people know you; you don't know yourself.
- Hope is allowed but **expensive** — water, clarity, refusing the drink.
- Sensory anchors: ash, bourbon, espresso bitterness, lamp soot, wool rug worn bare, cloud smell like wet stone.
- No twist that absolves the player without cost. If innocent, you were made to forget on purpose. If guilty, you chose the forgetting.

## Open Questions

- Is the player literally the same man each loop, or a **sequence of investigators** wearing one face?
- Does Graysmill ever appear on-screen, or stay a **mouth** the mountain speaks through?
- Who owns the cabin — company, bartender, cowboy, or you on a previous pass?
- What is on the slip of paper — and when do we let the player wish they had never unfolded it?
